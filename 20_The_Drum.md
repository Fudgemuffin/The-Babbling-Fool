# The Drum

The Dreaming is not past tense.

English forces a tense. "The Dreamtime." As if it happened once and ended. As if creation was a weekend project, finished and left behind.

The word has no tense. is. Continuous. Present. Operating. Not a time. An operation. Still running. Running now. Running when the first ancestor sang the first track into the ground. Running when you read this sentence.

We translated a living operation into a historical event. A present-tense verb into a past-tense noun. Turned a process into a thing that sits in a museum of ideas. The translation added a costume the original never wore.

The Dreaming wears no tense. We put one on it because our language could not hold it without one.

The songlines cross the continent.

Paths. Not roads. Not highways. Not lines on a map. Paths sung into the land by the ancestors who walked them. The walking is the singing is the creating is the maintaining. Not four actions. One.

A songline passes through the country of many peoples. Different languages. Different nations. Different stories. The same melody.

One people sings their section in their language. The songline crosses into the next people's country. They sing their section in their language. Different words. Same melody. The structure persists across every change.

...

Knowledge is in country.

Not stored in country. Not kept in country. Not filed in country the way a book is filed on a shelf. The place is the knowledge. The waterhole is what the waterhole teaches. The rock is what the rock holds.

You cannot learn it without walking to it. The walk is the learning. Not preparation for learning. Not the commute to the classroom. The walk itself, the heat, the thirst, the attention the land demands from the body moving through it. That is the knowledge arriving.

We separated information from location. Filed knowledge in books. Shelved books in libraries. Built libraries in cities. The knowledge sits on paper behind walls, disconnected from the ground it came from.

Alexandria burned. Baghdad burned. The books burned because books can burn.

The land did not burn away. The songlines did not burn away. Sixty-five thousand years. Ice ages. Sea level changes. The arrival of outsiders who burned everything they could reach.

The most resilient storage is not the most portable. The most resilient storage is the most embedded. Written into the thing itself. Not on it. In it. The knowledge and the land are not two things. The separation is the loss.

...

Initiation is compression.

Not ritual. Not ceremony. Compression of landscape into body.

The young person walks the songline. The country teaches. The physical act of walking the land under the conditions of walking. The heat entering through the skin. The ground entering through the feet. The distances entering through the eyes. The sounds entering through the ears. The body processes the landscape the way a lens processes light. Not by choosing what to resolve. By being shaped by what passes through it.

The initiated person is the country they walked. Not knows it. Not memorized it. is it. The body carries the country the way a riverbed carries the shape of the river that carved it.

Walking is knowing. This was never separated here. Never built a classroom and wondered why the knowledge felt thinner inside it.

...

This piece does not explain the Dreaming. The protocols exist. The knowledge is shared when the elders choose to share it. Not extracted. Not analyzed. Not decoded from outside by someone with a theory. Received. On the terms of those who carry it.

Sixty-five thousand years. The oldest continuous operation on the planet. Not because they preserved it. Because they never separated it from what it is.

...

Nobody beats the drum.
The drum is the beating.
Through whoever walks the country.
