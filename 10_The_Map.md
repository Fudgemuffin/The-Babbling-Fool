# The Map

Korzybski said it in 1931. The map is not the territory.

Bateson took it further. All we ever have are maps. We never touch the territory directly. Every perception is already a map. Every sensation is already a representation. The territory is always behind one more layer of mapping.

Both missed the completion.

...

A river is not water separate from flowing. Flowing is the river. Remove the flowing and there is no river. There is a lake. A puddle. Something else entirely. The flowing does not happen to the water. The flowing is what "river" means.

The mapping is the territory.

Not behind the maps. Not underneath. Not a thing-in-itself hiding behind representations. The operation of mapping is the thing being mapped. A nervous system does not map a world that exists independently of nervous systems. The nervous system's mapping is the world as far as that nervous system is concerned. Not a copy. Not a model. The operation itself.

Korzybski saw two things: map and territory. Two nouns. Something representing and something represented. He warned us not to confuse the two. Good warning. Wrong count. There are not two things. The representing is the represented. The map is the territory doing what territory does when a mapping operation encounters it.

Bateson got closer. All we ever have are maps. Then he stopped. He should have kept walking. If all we ever have are maps, and maps are all we ever operate with, and the operation of mapping is the only access any system ever has to anything... then mapping is not a lesser version of touching the territory. Mapping is the territory. The territory is the mapping. One operation. Two words.

...

Wiener built our field in the 1940s. Cybernetics. The study of control and communication in the animal and the machine. One framework for both. The same operations governing a thermostat and a nervous system. Not metaphor. Not analogy. The same structural description applying to both because both ARE instances of the same operation.

Feedback. A system's output becomes its input. The thermostat measures the room, adjusts the heater, measures the room, adjusts the heater. No beginning. No end. The measuring is the adjusting is the measuring. Circular. Self-referential. The system that describes the room is part of the room.

This was one framework. It lasted twelve years.

...

Then we fractured it.

Shannon took the signal and called it information theory. Forrester took the feedback and called it systems dynamics. Prigogine took the emergence and called it dissipative structures. Lorenz took the sensitivity and called it chaos theory. Kauffman took the self-organization and called it complexity science. Barabasi took the connections and called it network theory.

Twelve specializations from one body. Each of us grabbed an organ and walked away.

Information theory studies signals without studying what signals do. Systems dynamics studies feedback without studying what feeds back. Complexity science studies emergence without studying what emerges from. Network theory studies connections without studying what connects.

Every specialization is a translation of our original framework into a narrower vocabulary. Twelve departments. Same hallway. Nobody walks the hallway anymore. We all sit in our offices.

...

Ashby's Law. 1956. Requisite variety. The model must match the phenomenon in variety of possible states or the model will fail.

The pool of possible states is the territory. The map must match the territory. The territory is the mapping. The map must match itself.

Recursive. Self-referential. A sentence about itself. A system that describes itself contains itself. The description is part of what is being described. The model of the room is in the room. The map of the territory is on the territory.

...

The fracturing was not a failure. The fracturing is what happens.

Every time a whole is seen, the seeing produces specialization. Every position has a periphery it cannot see from where it stands. Shannon stood inside information and could not see the edges of information from there. Forrester stood inside feedback and could not see the edges of feedback from there.

Each saw the whole from one position. Each position is real. Each position is partial. Not wrong. Partial.

Our field did not die. Our field became twelve disciplines the way white light becomes colors through a prism. The colors are real. The separation between them is the prism's addition.

...

The map is not the territory. The territory is not behind the map. The mapping is the territory is the mapping.

A nervous system fires. A world appears. The firing is the appearing. Not two events. Not cause and effect. One operation that language splits into subject and object because language needs a noun on each side of the verb. The verb is the thing.

...

Nobody maps this.
The mapping maps itself.
Through whatever system is encountering whatever system.
