# The Equation

In any system, what flows in and what flows out balance.

Energy ratio. Matter ratio. Their ratio is one.

(Ei:Eo) / (Mi:Mo) = 1.

Conservation. Energy and matter. Nothing created. Nothing destroyed. Nothing lost. The sum is always one.

...

For bound physical systems, Clausius gave us the instrument. 1870. The virial theorem. For a system in equilibrium under an inverse-distance potential: 2K + U = 0.

At equilibrium, the flow IS the state. The kinetic energy at any instant equals the energy moving through the system. The potential energy at any instant equals the energy bound. K and |U| measure what Ei:Eo and Mi:Mo describe.

Normalize it.

Er = K / (K + |U|).
Mr = |U| / (K + |U|).

Er + Mr = 1.

The energy ratio and the matter ratio sum to one.

...

Define Density.

Density = Er / Mr.

At virial equilibrium, K = |U|/2. The theorem requires it.

Er = 1/3. Mr = 2/3. Density = 0.5.

Every bound system in equilibrium sits here. Not an approximation. The math permits no other value for a 1/r potential.

...

The Sun. Binding: ~2.3 x 10⁴¹ J. Thermal: ~1.1 x 10⁴¹ J. Er = 0.333. Mr = 0.667. Density = 0.5 (Cox, 2000). Bound. Stable. 4.6 billion years.

The hydrogen atom. Coulomb potential. Same 1/r. Kinetic: 13.6 eV. Binding: 27.2 eV. Er = 0.333. Mr = 0.667. Density = 0.5 (NIST CODATA 2018). Most abundant atom in the universe.

One is gravitational. One is electromagnetic. Same ratio. A star and an atom at the same structural position.

...

At threshold, K = |U|.

Er = 0.5. Mr = 0.5. Density = 1.

The ratio of what moves to what holds equals one. Transition.

Jeans instability. Thermal equals gravitational. Cloud collapses into star (Jeans, 1902). Density = 1.

Phase transition. Thermal equals binding. Ice melts (Atkins). Density = 1.

Curie temperature. Thermal reaches exchange energy. Iron: 0.090 eV thermal, ~0.1 eV exchange (Kittel, Ch. 12). Density ~ 1. Magnetism breaks.

Iron. Fe-56. Binding energy per nucleon peaks at 8.8 MeV. Below iron, fusion releases energy. Above iron, fission releases energy. At iron, neither. Stars die here (Weizsacker, 1935; B²FH, 1957). Density = 1.

Event horizon. Escape velocity equals light speed. K = |U| (Schwarzschild, 1916). Density = 1.

...

Every tipping point we have ever measured is Density equals one.

Gravitational collapse. Phase transition. Magnetic ordering. Nuclear binding. Event horizon.

Five phenomena. One ratio.

Testable. Take any bound system. Measure K and |U|. Compute Er, Mr, Density. If Density = 0.5 at equilibrium and 1 at transition, correct. If not, wrong.

The virial theorem. A century and a half. Never normalized as a ratio. Never compared across scales.

...

Nobody wrote this equation.
The ratio was always one.
Through whatever system is bound.
