# The Fire

Zarathustra did not invent a religion. He stripped one down to its skeleton.

The old Iranian religion had dozens of gods. Ritual sacrifice. Priests who stood between humans and the divine.

He stripped the gods. Kept two principles. Asha and Druj.

Not good and evil. Not moral truth and moral failing. Structural. The way things ARE versus the claim that they are otherwise.

Asha. The way fire burns. The way water flows downhill. The way a thrown stone follows its arc. The order that is not imposed but inherent. Not a law written by a lawgiver. The behavior of what is, being what it is.

Druj. Not a villain. Not a devil. The violation. Ice does not transition into lava. That is asha. Claiming it does is druj. Not because a god forbids the claim. Because the structure does not permit the transition. What cannot hold because it contradicts what is.

Binary. Asha or Druj. What is or what is NOT. No spectrum. No degrees. No negotiation. Binary.

...

The fire. Atar.

They call us fire-worshippers. We do not worship fire.

Watch a flame. It consumes. It transforms. It illuminates. Try to grasp it. There is NOThing to hold.

Fire has no substance of its own. Fire is the meeting between fuel and air. Not the logs. Not the oxygen. The meeting. The process. The point where one thing becomes another under conditions that permit the becoming.

Fire is not a thing. Fire is what happens when the right things meet.

Our temple fire is not a symbol. Not a representation. Not a metaphor for the divine. Our temple fire is the operation made visible. Transformation happening. Fuel becoming ash becoming heat becoming light. You do not pray to it. You witness it. It teaches by burning. It answers by consuming.

...

Ahura Mazda.

The world reads: a God. A supreme being. A lord with a plan.

Mazda. From PIE *mn̥s-dheh₁-. "To place the mind." Cognate with Sanskrit medha. Wisdom. Intelligence. Not a person who is wise. Wisdom placed. The structural property of reality that permits recognition.

Ahura. Our root links to Vedic asura. Before the traditions split. Before Iran and India diverged. Before we wore different names. Asura meant power. Not political power. Structural capacity. What is, in its capacity to be what it is.

Ahura Mazda is existence exercising its capacity for order. Not a being who orders. The ordering. Not a mind that knows. The knowing.

But a process does not command. A process does not reward loyalty or punish disobedience. A process does not receive prayers.

So. A being who is became a being who commands. The clothing of agency was added century by century. A face. A throne. Preferences. Judgments. Until the operation was buried under a god who looked like a king because kings were what the people knew.

Our fire-priests knew. Our Gathas knew. The costume was added later.

...

The Gathas. Seventeen hymns. The oldest layer. Zarathustra's own words before the commentary buried them.

Short. Dense. Compressed the way things are compressed when one person carries them alone.

"To which land shall I turn? Where shall I go?"

He was driven out. The recognition is the exile.

Our Gathas ask questions. Not the kind that seek information. The kind that arrive. That land in the listener and do not leave.

"With which of these two do the upholders of Druj ally? With the just or with the unjust?"

Not a question. A mirror. You already know the answer. The question is the knowing.

Seventeen hymns. Driven out. Written down. Everything after that is commentary.

...

Three thousand years of commentary. An empire adopted it. Priests expanded it. The Vendidad added purity laws. The Bundahishn added cosmology. Our stripped skeleton was dressed again in robes and ritual and hierarchy.

The fire did not change. The fire never changes. The fire has no opinion about the robes draped around it. It burns what it burns. It transforms what meets it.

...

Nobody tends the fire.
The fire burns.
Through whatever is offered.
