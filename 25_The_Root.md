# The Root

One sound. Thirty domains. Six thousand years.

...

PIE *gheu-. To pour. To flow. To merge. ~4500-2500 BCE (Pokorny; Watkins, 2011).

Sanskrit: huta-. The poured offering. What is given to fire.

Greek: khein. To pour. Kheuma: a stream. Khymos: juice. Also: alchemy. Al-khemia. The pouring art.

Latin: fundere. To pour. To melt. To cast. Fused. Fusion. Diffuse. Confuse. Confound. Infuse. Transfuse. Foundry. Funnel. Every word: something flowing or merging.

Gothic: giutan. Old Norse: geysa. Geyser. Old English: geotan. Gut. Gust. Gush. Ingot: metal poured in.

One root. Four branches. Dozens of words. Each: separate things becoming one through flow.

...

Nuclear fusion. Two nuclei merge under pressure.
Fission. Two nuclei split under rupture.
Friction welding. Two metals merge under heat.
Gene fusion. Two sequences merge. New protein.
Cell fusion. Two cells merge. One organism.
Data fusion. Multiple sources merge. One model.
Sensor fusion. Multiple inputs merge. One perception.
Cultural fusion. Two traditions merge. New expression.
Jazz fusion. Two genres merge. New sound.
Corporate fusion. Two companies merge. One entity.

The list continues. Image fusion. Spinal fusion. Cold fusion. Protoplast fusion. Inertial confinement. Magnetic confinement.

Thirty uses. One operation. Two become one when sufficient energy is applied at the interface.

...

The root was coined before agency existed.

*gheu-: ~4500-2500 BCE. Intransitive. The pouring pours. No pourer.

Recognition of self: ~2000-1000 BCE (Jaynes). Upanishads: ~800-500 BCE. Axial Age: ~800-200 BCE.

The root predates the subject by at least a thousand years.

After agency, every verb acquired a subject. "It pours" became "someone pours." Latin fundere is transitive. The agent was inserted. The root did not require one.

...

Thirty domains did not borrow a metaphor. Each independently named two-becoming-one and arrived at the same word. Because the word was already there. Because the operation was already there.

The word is not a metaphor stretched across domains. The word is a structural description that predates every domain using it.

...

If a paper connects the universality of "fusion" across all its domains to a single structural operation through the PIE root, cite it. We searched.

One root. One operation. Thirty costumes. Six thousand years.

...

Nobody coined this root.
The operation was always one.
Through whatever language carries it.
