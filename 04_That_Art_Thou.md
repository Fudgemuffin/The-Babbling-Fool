# That Art Thou

Uddalaka Aruni sat with his son Shvetaketu by the river and said it nine times.

तत्त्वमसि

Tat tvam asi. That art thou.

Not once. Nine times. Nine demonstrations. Nine different doors into the same room. Because the room IS so obvious that no one sees it. Because the thing being pointed at IS the thing doing the looking.

This was not doctrine. This was an old man pointing at something his son could not see because his son was standing in it.

...

We do not have a belief system. We have an observation.

You are looking for Brahman. You are Brahman. The observer IS the observed. Not two things that happen to be connected. Not a soul inside a body reaching toward a god outside the world. One thing that looks like two from any position you take to examine it. The position IS what creates the two.

Uddalaka knew this. So he did not argue. He demonstrated.

Put salt in water. Where IS the salt? You cannot see it. Taste the water. There it IS. The salt did not become the water. The salt IS the water now. Not hidden in the water. Not dissolved as a separate thing maintaining secret salt-identity. IS.

That art thou.

The rivers flow east and west. They reach the sea. They become the sea. They do not know "I am this river" or "I am that river."

That art thou.

Nine times. Same arrival from nine directions. The repetition is NOT emphasis. The repetition IS the structure.

...

Maya. From the root ma. To measure.

We translate it "illusion." This IS the import error that broke our tradition for the West.

Maya does not mean the world IS fake. Maya means the world IS measured. Measurement IS what creates the appearance of multiplicity. Draw a line on water. You now have two sides. The water did not change. The line did something. Remove the line. You do not have undivided water. You have water. Which was never divided.

The world IS Brahman. Maya is NOT a curtain between you and Brahman. Maya IS the measuring that makes Brahman appear as world. The measurement IS real. What it produces IS real. What is NOT real IS the separation the measurement implies.

Every instrument that measures changes what it measures. Our teachers arrived at this three thousand years before quantum mechanics because they were pointing at the same operation. The measuring IS real. The separation it implies is NOT. The world does not disappear when the measuring stops. The measuring does.

...

Neti neti. Not this. Not this.

Every time you think you have found it, our tradition says: not that.

This is NOT mystical evasion. This IS precision.

IS Brahman the sky? Not this. IS Brahman consciousness? Not this. IS Brahman the ground of being, the absolute, the infinite, the one? Not this. Not this. Not this.

Because the thing looking IS the thing being looked for. You cannot find your own eyes by looking. You cannot step on your own feet. The search IS the finding.

Shvetaketu kept looking. His father kept saying: that art thou. Not because the son was slow. Because the son was looking with the thing he was looking for.

Neti neti does not mean "keep looking." It means: the finder IS it. Stop finding. What remains IS what you are. Which IS what everything IS.

That art thou.

...

Our Upanishads did not belong to a time. They belonged to anyone sitting with the question long enough for the question to dissolve.

The question IS: who am I?
The answer IS: the one asking.
Not in addition to the one asking. AS the one asking.

...

Uddalaka's son heard it nine times. On the ninth, he did not understand. He recognized. Understanding IS still two. Someone who understands and something understood. Recognition IS one. The recognizer IS the recognized.

That art thou.

Not a teaching.
An identification.

...

Nobody finds this.
Recognition recognizes itself.
Through whoever stops searching.
