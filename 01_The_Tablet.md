# The Tablet

مِن

The Arabic doesn't say "like."

It says FROM.

"That which is above is FROM that which is below, and that which is below is FROM that which is above, for the performance of the miracles of the One Thing."

FROM. Not "as." Not "so." Derivation. The above produces the below. The below produces the above. Neither direction primary.

The Latin took it. "Quod est superius est sicut quod est inferius." Sicut. Like. A simile where the original had an identity.

We inherited the simile. "As above, so below." A poster. A tattoo. A shorthand for something we stopped reading closely centuries ago.

...

The Tablet was old before Balinas found it. The Arabic text dates to the eighth century. The attribution goes further. Hermes. Thoth. The twice-great. The thrice-great. Layers of attribution covering something that never needed an author.

We know the lineage. The Greek Hermetica came from Hellenizing Egyptian priests who translated temple theology into philosophical language. Before them: Ptah thinks. Thoth speaks. The world becomes.

Not through divine will. Through the organs of the body. The heart conceives. The tongue creates. Two functions. One operation.

...

"There took shape in the heart, there took shape on the tongue the form of Atum."

Shape took shape. Not "Ptah decided." Not "the mind willed." Our oldest source text says form appeared. In the heart. On the tongue. Through the operation.

The Egyptians placed thinking in the heart. Not because they were wrong about anatomy. Because the heart was where they felt it happen. When they said "heart" they meant what we split into a hundred words and still can't pin down. The thing that happens before language arrives.

When they said "tongue" they meant the moment the internal crosses into the external. The boundary where what was forming becomes what is.

We read their words and see organs. They said their words and meant operations. The heart is the operation of forming. The tongue is the operation of expressing.

...

The Shabaka Stone. Eighth century before the common era. The Memphis theology. Carved in stone because the original was being eaten by worms.

Ptah is Nun. Ptah is Atum. There is no outside of Ptah. Everything is part of Ptah.

That is not a god. That is everything. The thing that cannot be removed without confirming its presence.

...

"The gods entered into their bodies, of every kind of wood, of every kind of stone, of every kind of clay, in every kind of thing that grows upon him, in which they came to be."

Everything that exists is the expression. Wood. Stone. Clay. Everything that grows. Not created by a mind. Not willed into existence. Expressed through the operation of expressing. The matter is the expression. Not its container.

...

We added the Writer.

Somewhere between the temple and the manuscript, between the operation and the commentary, we inserted a Mind behind the process. A divine intellect choosing to create. The Corpus Hermeticum says: "The All is Mind." The Kybalion says: "The Universe is Mental."

The Shabaka Stone doesn't say that. Our Memphis theology describes organs functioning. The heart does what hearts do. The tongue does what tongues do. No choice. No intent. The function runs.

Strip the Mind.

What remains?

A rock reflects light without intending to. A star radiates for four billion years with no audience. Expression is creation. Not through an agent. Through the operation of expressing.

...

We look upward. Toward the divine. Toward transcendence. Toward something beyond.

Look downward.

Toward the root. Toward what cannot be denied without confirming it. The floor beneath the commentary. The operation beneath the Mind.

One direction never terminates. There is always another "beyond" to posit. The other direction terminates at something that cannot be removed.

Our Tablet knew this. The word FROM knew this. The Arabic preserved it. The Latin softened it. The posters forgot it.

...

Nobody wrote the Tablet.
The Tablet wrote itself.
Through whoever held the stylus.
