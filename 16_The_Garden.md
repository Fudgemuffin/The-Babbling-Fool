# The Garden

What IS the sound of one hand clapping?

This is NOT a riddle. A riddle has an answer you have not found yet. This IS an instruction. Stop dividing. Before the clap, before two hands, before the sound, before the one who hears the sound. Before subject and object. Before the question and the one asking.

Our koans do not ask you to think harder. They ask you to stop being the one solving. The answer is NOT a thought. The answer IS what IS already happening before you split it into question and questioner.

A monk can sit with this for thirty years and find nothing. Not because the monk IS slow. Because the monk IS still the monk sitting with it. The koan waits for the monk to stop.

座禅。

Zazen. Sitting.

We say: sit in order to reach enlightenment. This imports a sitter, a sitting, a reaching, and an enlightenment. Four things. Four actors on a stage.

Dogen said: sitting IS enlightenment. Not leads to. Not produces. Not develops into. IS.

The practice IS the goal. The sitting IS the arrival. There is NO distance between the cushion and what the cushion IS for. The cushion IS what it IS for.

Hui Neng. Sixth Patriarch. Illiterate.

He was pounding rice in the monastery kitchen when the Fifth Patriarch announced a contest. Write a poem demonstrating your understanding. Shenxiu, the head monk, had studied for decades. He wrote:

The body IS the Bodhi tree.
The mind IS like a clear mirror standing.
Polish it diligently.
Do not let dust collect.

Gradual. Careful. Earned. A lifetime of study in four clean lines.

Hui Neng could not write. He asked someone to write for him:

There IS no Bodhi tree.
Nor stand of a mirror bright.
Since all IS void from the beginning,
where can dust alight?

Not because Hui Neng was gifted. Because he had no framework in the way. Shenxiu had forty years of words between him and the operation. Forty years of polishing. Forty years of mirrors and dust and trees. The words were the distance.

Hui Neng heard one line of the Diamond Sutra while carrying firewood. One line. He recognized what the monastery could not in forty years. The rice pounder had nothing between him and the pounding. We had libraries.

"Abide nowhere. Let the mind arise."

Not: empty the mind. Not: achieve stillness. Not: find the quiet place beneath the noise.

Abide NOWHERE. No position. Not this position or that position or the special position of no-position. Nowhere.

Let the mind arise. Not: produce a state. Not: cultivate awareness. Let. The arising IS the mind IS the letting. One operation. The mind does not arise because you let it. The letting IS the arising IS the mind.

We hear "abide nowhere" and try to abide nowhere. The trying IS the abiding IS the somewhere. The instruction dissolves the one receiving the instruction. The way sleep does not come by trying to sleep. Sleep comes when trying stops. What remains IS what was always running.

A monk asked Zhaozhou: does a dog have Buddha nature?

Zhaozhou said: Wu.

Not yes. Not no. Not nothing. Wu. The sound before the question was a question. The answer that predates the asking. Does a dog have Buddha nature. The question assumes a dog. Assumes a nature. Assumes a having. Three things. Wu IS what IS there before the three.

We rake the garden every morning. The lines in the gravel are not permanent. The raking IS the garden. Not the lines. The lines wash away. The raking continues. We do not rake to create beauty. The raking IS the beauty IS the monk IS the morning.

You visit and photograph the lines. You miss everything. The lines are what was left after the raking stopped. The raking was the thing.

What IS there.
