# The Wave

Before the word "collapse," something was happening.

A pool of admissible states. Not a cloud of real things. Not a fog of simultaneous existences. A pool. The shape of what has not yet been determined. The way a coin in the air is not heads and tails simultaneously. The coin in the air is undetermined. The pool is the shape of the not-yet.

The coin lands. One state. The pool narrows to a point.

We called this "collapse." Our word imports destruction. A building collapses. A bridge collapses. Something real falls apart. The word tells you there was a structure that existed and then was destroyed.

Nothing was destroyed.

A pool of admissible states narrowed to one admissible state. The narrowing is not destruction. The narrowing is determination. What was undetermined became determined. No building fell. No structure was lost. The question was answered. That is all.

The pool was never a physical state. The pool was the shape of what we did not know yet.

...

The double-slit experiment. 1801. Thomas Young.

Light through two slits. On the wall behind: an interference pattern. Bands of light and dark. The pattern of two waves overlapping. Light is a wave.

Send photons one at a time. Single particles. One through the slits. Then another. Then another. On the wall behind: the same interference pattern. Built up particle by particle. Each photon lands in one spot. But over thousands of photons, the spots form the wave pattern.

Each photon is determined upon arrival. The pattern of arrivals is the wave. The wave is not a thing that travels. The wave is the shape of how determination distributes over many determinations. The wave is statistical. The particle is individual. Both descriptions are complete. Neither is more real.

We forced a question: is it a wave or a particle? The answer IS: it is a determination. "Wave" describes the pattern of many determinations. "Particle" describes one determination. Different descriptions at different scales.

...

"Observer."

The word that broke a century of our understanding.

A photon's state is undetermined until interaction. We called the interaction "observation." Our word imports consciousness. An observer is someone who watches. Someone. Human. Aware. The interpretation followed the word: consciousness collapses the wave function. The human mind determines reality.

None of this is in our physics.

The operation is interaction. Any system interacting with any other system resolves uncertainty. A photon hitting a detector. A particle encountering a magnetic field. A rock hitting water. Each is an interaction. Each resolves a previously undetermined state into a determined one. No consciousness required. No awareness. No watching.

A Geiger counter determines a radioactive decay event. The counter is not conscious. The counter interacts. The interaction is the determination. We imported the word "observer" from a world where humans were the center of every sentence. Our physics has no center. Our physics has interactions.

...

Entanglement.

Einstein called it "spooky action at a distance." The name stuck. The understanding did not.

Two particles interact. They separate. Measure one. The other's state is instantly determined. No matter the distance. Faster than light. Spooky.

Nothing traveled.

The road from Athens to Thebes and the road from Thebes to Athens are one road. Measure the road from the Athens end: 75 kilometers. Measure the road from the Thebes end: 75 kilometers. The measurements agree instantly. Not because Athens sent a signal to Thebes. Because the road is one road.

Entangled particles are one state measured from two positions. There were never two things. There was one state. The two-ness is the measurement's addition.

Nothing was communicated. Nothing was sent. One state. Two readings. The readings agree because they read the same page.

...

Einstein said: God does not play dice.

Bohr said: Stop telling God what to do.

Both wrong. Both imported a player.

Einstein imported a God who would not gamble and a universe that was secretly determined. Bohr imported a God with preferences and a universe that was fundamentally random. Dice implies a game. A game implies a player. The universe is not playing.

A pool narrows. What occurred is what occurred. No player chose the outcome. No dice were rolled. No game was played. A state was undetermined. An interaction occurred. The state determined.

...

Schrodinger's cat is not in two states.

The cat is undetermined. From outside the box. The box has not been opened. No interaction has resolved the pool of admissible states for the outside observer.

Inside the box, the interaction has already occurred. The atom decayed or did not. The detector fired or did not. The cat is alive or dead. Determined. The determination happened at the interaction. Not at the opening of the box.

The cat is not in superposition. The outside observer's knowledge is in superposition. When the box opens, the observer's knowledge narrows. The cat does not change. The observer's pool does.

Schrodinger designed the thought experiment to show how absurd the Copenhagen interpretation was. He was right. It is absurd to say the cat is in two states. The cat is in one state. The pool is the observer's, not the cat's.

Our word "superposition" dressed the observer's ignorance in the cat's clothing. The cat never wore it.

...

The wave function is not a thing in the world. The wave function is a description of the pool of admissible states given what is known. New information narrows the pool. The narrowing is determination. Not destruction. Not collapse. Not magic. Not consciousness. Determination.

Every measurement is an interaction. Every interaction is a determination. Every determination narrows a pool.

Our physics contained interactions and determinations. Our words dressed these in a century of costume.

Systems interact. States determine. Pools narrow. No player. No dice. No game. No spookiness. No cat that is two things.

...

Nobody collapses anything.
The determination determines itself.
Through whatever interaction is occurring.
