# The Seed

Darwin did not discover survival of the fittest. Herbert Spencer coined the phrase. Darwin used "natural selection." Spencer was a sociologist. He needed winners. Darwin needed a mechanism. Both reached for a word. Both words imported what was not there.

Selection implies a selector. Natural implies a nature doing the selecting. Our phrase "natural selection" contains two agents. Neither exists.

Strip the agents. What remains.

Configurations that fit the conditions persist. Configurations that do not, do not. Binary. No selector. No aim. No preference. Filtering. The way a sieve does not choose which grains pass through. The holes are a size. The grains are a size. What fits, fits. What does not, does not. The sieve did nothing. The sieve is.

...

"Nature selects." Parse that. A nature. A selection. Two nouns. Two agents. Two actors on a stage performing a drama called Evolution.

There is no nature. There is no selection. What happens is what happens. What persists is what fit. What did not fit is gone. We describe this as if the environment reviewed applications and chose winners. The environment did nothing. The environment is. What is admissible within it persists. What is not admissible does not.

No one shapes the river. The shape is.

...

A seed falls. Lands in shade. Does not germinate. Another seed falls. Lands in light. Germinates. Grows.

The light did not select the second seed. The shade did not reject the first. The conditions were different. The outcomes were different. The condition is the outcome. One thing described as two because we stand outside it and see a before and an after.

...

Mutation. We call it random. It is not random. It is undetermined from our position. We do not know which nucleotide will change. But the change is physical. A photon hits a molecule. A chemical bond shifts. A copying enzyme misreads. Every "random" mutation has a cause. We are ignorant of the cause. We are not witnessing randomness. We are witnessing our own resolution limit.

"Random" is a word for where our measurement stops. The mutation is not random to the photon. The photon went exactly where it went. We call it random because we did not track the photon. The word describes us. Not the event.

...

Adaptation. We say organisms adapt to their environment. As if they try. As if they notice. As if there is an intent to fit.

Organisms do not adapt. Organisms that happen to fit persist. The ones that do not, do not. The fitting was never a verb. It was always a filter. Our word "adaptation" imports an adapter the way our word "selection" imports a selector. Neither is there.

A lock does not adapt to a key. A key does not adapt to a lock. The key that fits turns. The keys that do not fit do not. Pile up a billion keys. The ones that turned are still in the lock. That is the fossil record. Not a story of trying. A record of fitting.

...

Convergent evolution. Eyes evolved independently at least forty times. Wings at least four times. Echolocation at least twice.

Forty separate lineages. Forty different starting points. Forty different paths through the filter. Same structure at the end.

Not because evolution "aims" at eyes. Not because light-sensing is optimal. Not because the universe has preferences. Because the conditions of navigating an environment saturated with electromagnetic radiation make light-sensing structures admissible. Everywhere those conditions exist, the same filter runs.

The conditions ARE the eye. Forty paths. One arrival. The eye is what the electromagnetic spectrum looks like when biology passes through it. The wing is what gravity and atmosphere look like when biology passes through them.

...

We split our own field from physics. Biology is "life." Physics is "matter." Different departments. Different journals. Different tenure tracks.

A cell is physics. A membrane is a boundary condition. Diffusion is propagation. Homeostasis is bounded oscillation. Replication is compression. Mutation is expansion.

There are no departments. There is one operation at different densities. We split the departments because our institutions had departments.

A river does not know it is hydrology. A cell does not know it is biology. The names are ours. The operation is the operation.

...

Nobody selects.
The filter runs.
Through whatever fits the conditions.
