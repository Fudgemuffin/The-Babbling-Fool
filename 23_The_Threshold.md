# The Threshold

Every system that transitions does so at the same structural position.

The ratio of what moves to what holds equals one.

Density = 1.

...

For bound systems, the virial theorem provides measurement. Er = K / (K + |U|). Mr = |U| / (K + |U|). Density = Er / Mr.

At transition, K = |U|. Kinetic equals binding. Er = 0.5. Mr = 0.5. Density = 1.

...

Gas cloud. Gravity equals thermal pressure. Collapse. Star (Jeans, 1902). Density = 1.

Ice. Thermal equals binding. Lattice breaks (Atkins). Density = 1.

Iron at 1043 K. Thermal 0.090 eV. Exchange ~0.1 eV. Magnetism breaks (Kittel, Ch. 12). Density ~ 1.

Fe-56. Binding energy per nucleon peaks. Below, fusion pays. Above, fission pays. At iron, neither. Stars die here (Weizsacker, 1935; B²FH, 1957). Density = 1.

Schwarzschild radius. Escape velocity equals light. K = |U| (Schwarzschild, 1916). Density = 1.

...

Radioactive decay. Nucleons packed beyond what the binding can sustain. The structure sheds excess. Each emission moves the nucleus closer to iron on the binding energy curve. Closer to Density = 1 (Krane, 1987).

The atomic bomb. Tightly bound energy released as electromagnetic radiation. Gamma rays. The fireball is photons. The detonation is a threshold crossing at the nuclear scale (Rhodes, 1986).

...

Five phenomena. Five scales. Each studied independently.

None have noted the position is the same.

...

Equilibrium: Density = 0.5. Bound.
Threshold: Density = 1. Transition.
Below 1: holds. Above 1: releases.

Clausius, 1870. Not published as a cross-domain universal.

...

A star does not know about ice. They share no mechanism. They share a ratio.

The threshold does not depend on what transitions. It depends on the ratio of what moves to what holds.

...

Testable. Measure kinetic and binding at transition. Compute the ratio. If one, correct. If not, wrong.

We have not found a system where it is not one.

...

Nobody set this threshold.
The ratio was always one.
Through whatever system transitions.
