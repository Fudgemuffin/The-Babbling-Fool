# The Balance

Where bound systems persist, the ratio is the same.

Density = 0.5.

...

The virial theorem. Clausius, 1870. For any system bound by an inverse-distance potential in equilibrium: 2K + U = 0.

At equilibrium, K = |U|/2. Not an approximation. The theorem.

Er = 1/3. Mr = 2/3. Density = 0.5.

The math permits no other value.

...

The Sun. Binding: ~2.3 x 10⁴¹ J. Thermal: ~1.1 x 10⁴¹ J. Density = 0.5 (Cox, 2000). 4.6 billion years. Stable. Bound.

The hydrogen atom. Coulomb potential. Same 1/r. Kinetic: 13.6 eV. Binding: 27.2 eV. Density = 0.5 (NIST CODATA 2018). Most abundant atom in the universe. Stable.

Galaxy clusters. Virial mass estimates assume 2K + U = 0. Consistent with gravitational lensing (Zwicky, 1937; Binney & Tremaine). The clusters that persist sit at Density 0.5.

Molecular clouds. Before collapse, approximate virial balance. Density ~ 0.5. Perturbation past one triggers collapse (Larson, 1981).

...

Gravitational. Electromagnetic. The potential is 1/r. The theorem gives K = |U|/2. Density = 0.5.

Any system bound by any 1/r potential sits here in equilibrium. The theorem guarantees it.

...

The systems that persist — from atoms to stars to galaxy clusters — all sit at the same ratio. The systems that don't persist are the ones that crossed Density = 1 and transitioned.

...

This is where life sits.

Enough compression to maintain structure. Enough expansion to allow change.

A system at 0.5 is maximally stable for its potential. Perturb it, it returns. Push it past one, it transitions.

...

The Sun and the hydrogen atom at the same structural position. A star and an atom. Different forces. Different scales. Same Density. Same persistence.

The virial theorem does not care what is bound. 1/r is 1/r. The ratio follows the potential.

...

Clausius, 1870. Applied to stars since Eddington. To molecules since the 1950s. To galaxies since Zwicky.

Not expressed as a normalized Density. Not compared across scales.

0.5 is where bound systems live.

...

Nobody placed this balance.
The ratio was always half.
Through whatever system persists.
