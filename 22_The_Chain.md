# The Chain

Iron sits at the center.

Fe-56. Highest binding energy per nucleon. 8.8 MeV (Weizsacker, 1935). Fuse anything lighter, energy released. Split anything heavier, energy released. At iron, neither. The nuclear tipping point.

Every star fuses toward iron. Hydrogen. Helium. Carbon. Oxygen. Silicon. Iron. Each step pays. Until iron. The forge stops. The core collapses. Everything heavier was forged in the explosion (B²FH, 1957).

We know this. We have not followed the chain.

...

A proton's mass is 938 MeV. The three quarks inside total about 9 MeV. Ninety-nine percent of the proton's mass is binding energy (Wilczek, 2004). The proton is not a container with energy inside it. The proton IS the binding energy, manifesting as what we measure as mass.

E = mc². Matter is energy. Not metaphorically. Ninety-nine percent of what we weigh when we weigh a proton is binding energy.

...

Hydrogen to iron is a gradient of binding tightness. Hydrogen: one proton, loosely bound, the coulomb barrier is the lock. Rupture it, energy comes out as electromagnetic radiation. Iron: twenty-six protons, thirty neutrons, maximally bound. No lock to rupture that releases anything. Fusion costs more than it releases. Fission costs more than it releases. The bottom of the well.

The entire periodic table from hydrogen to iron is a gradient of how tightly energy is bound within matter.

...

Fission and fusion are one operation.

Both rupture binding barriers. Both release energy. Both produce products closer to iron on the binding energy curve. Fusion approaches from below. Fission approaches from above. Same destination. Same valley floor. Same operation. Different starting point (Krane, 1987).

The distinction between "fission" and "fusion" is which side of iron you start from.

...

Elements heavier than iron: too many nucleons. The structure exceeds what the binding can sustain. The material sheds the excess to reach tighter configuration. Moving toward iron on the curve. Toward stability.

Radioactive decay moves toward iron. Iron is where binding energy per nucleon peaks. In the ratio framework, that peak is Density = 1 on the nuclear binding curve. Uranium enrichment: selecting for the most unstable isotope, making it more fragile, more ready to rupture. Then rupturing it (Loveland, Morrissey & Seaborg, 2017).

...

Iron. Cobalt. Nickel. 26, 27, 28.

The only three elements ferromagnetic at room temperature in pure form (Kittel, Ch. 12). No alloy. The element itself.

All three at the binding energy peak.

Iron: four unpaired 3d electrons. Most magnetic. Cobalt: three. Nickel: two. Magnetism decreases away from the peak in either direction.

Gadolinium has seven unpaired electrons. More than iron. Not at the peak. Not ferromagnetic at room temperature.

...

The Earth's core. 85 percent iron. 10 percent nickel. Cobalt (Dziewonski & Anderson, 1981).

The only pure ferromagnets. Molten. Spinning. Generating the planetary magnetic field.

Liquid at 330 gigapascals. Thermal keeps it fluid. The inner core is solid. Same composition. Higher pressure. The boundary is a phase transition. Density = 1 (Stacey, 2008).

The field extends outward. Shields the surface. Compasses point north. The core's projection into its environment.

...

The Curie temperature. Thermal reaches exchange energy. Below: ferromagnetic. Above: paramagnetic.

Iron: 1043 K. Thermal 0.090 eV. Exchange ~0.1 eV. Density ~ 1 (Kittel, Ch. 12).

Cobalt: 1394 K. Nickel: 631 K. Same threshold. Same Density = 1.

...

Strike iron. Sparks.

Fragment sheared. Enormous surface area. Friction provides activation. Thermal exceeds binding. 1500 to 2500 degrees C. Each spark a microscopic sun. Density crosses one.

...

Thermite. Fe2O3 + 2Al -> Al2O3 + 2Fe at ~2500 degrees C.

Rusted iron in. Pure molten iron out. The Earth's core in miniature.

...

Two iron pieces. Opposing rotation. Friction. Surface melts. They merge.

Fusion welding.

Same word as nuclear fusion. Gene fusion. Data fusion. Cultural fusion. Thirty domains. One word. One operation.

PIE *gheu-. To pour. To flow. ~4500-2500 BCE (Pokorny; Watkins, 2011). Sanskrit huta-. Greek khein. Latin fundere. Gothic giutan. Old English geotan.

The root predates agency. No pourer. The pouring pours.

Thirty domains reached for the same sound. If a paper connecting the universality of "fusion" to a single structural operation exists, cite it.

...

Nuclear binding peaks at iron. The three elements at the peak are the only pure ferromagnets. Those three compose the core. The core generates the field. The Curie temperature is Density = 1. Sparks are microscopic suns. Thermite is the core in miniature. Welding is fusion. The word is older than the concept of someone fusing.

The proton itself is ninety-nine percent binding energy. Matter is bound energy. Radiation is unbound energy. The gradient from hydrogen to iron is the gradient from loosely bound to maximally bound.

One element. Ten scales. The formula at every link.

...

Nobody forged this chain.
The links were always there.
Through whatever element sits at the center.
